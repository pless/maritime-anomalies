Scene-conditioned maritime pattern-of-life

Behavioral normalcy is conditional on place — and place can be encoded from static, chart-like geography (bathymetry, charted channels) instead of accumulated traffic history. One shared 8-word "place vocabulary" learned from seafloor geometry with zero traffic data, small trajectory models trained on Puget Sound + SF Bay and then frozen, everything below evaluated on Hampton Roads / Chesapeake — a port the models never saw. Three demos, three timescales:

01Retrospective anomalies, scene-explained
The forensic analysis: what's still anomalous after charted channels fix the bathymetry's blind spot, and why the scene says so. Every alert carries observed motion, the model's contextual expectation, a counterfactual "would be normal in ⟨place⟩," and conformal confidence. Click a card — the maneuver animates over ~20 km of tokenized scene. The full project story is at the bottom of this page.
211 raw → 107 genuine alerts, every removal explained 3 named false-positive classes
02Live threat assessment (causal)
The same frozen model run the way a watchstander system must: every minute, each vessel's most recent 12 minutes scored against its own past — no future context. Six-hour replay of the busiest watch, with a key methodological finding: thresholds must be calibrated against the live decision rule, or per-window calibration floods the display (44% of benign tracks firing).
15 alerts / 360 tracks in 6 h — at the calibrated ≤5% watch FAR 101K causal scores · ~30 s CPU
03Multi-hour behaviors (long-timescale arm)
A 256-step, 2-minute-token transformer (~8.5 h context) recognizes what a vessel has been doing for hours: stop-and-go pot/trap tending, slow area-working, shoreline passage, directed transit — clusters fit on the training ports reappear, signatures matching, in the never-seen Chesapeake. Long context pays exactly where behavior has long structure; directional look-ahead scene cues make feature-following turns predictable.
rhythmic work: −7.1% NLL from 8× more history fishing-like classes enriched 2.5–3.8×, assigned cold
