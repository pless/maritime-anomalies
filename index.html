Scene-conditioned maritime pattern-of-life

Behavioral normalcy is conditional on place — and place can be encoded from static, chart-like geography (bathymetry, charted channels) instead of accumulated traffic history. One shared 8-word "place vocabulary" learned from seafloor geometry with zero traffic data, small trajectory models trained on Puget Sound + SF Bay and then frozen, everything below evaluated on Hampton Roads / lower Chesapeake Bay — a region the models never saw. Four demos across timescales:

01Retrospective anomalies, scene-explained
The forensic analysis: what's still anomalous after charted channels fix the bathymetry's blind spot, and why the scene says so. Every alert carries observed motion, the model's contextual expectation, a counterfactual "would be normal in ⟨place⟩," and conformal confidence. Click a card — the maneuver animates over ~20 km of tokenized scene. The full project story is at the bottom of this page.
211 raw → 107 genuine alerts, every removal explained 3 named false-positive classes
02Live threat assessment (causal)
The same frozen model run the way a watchstander system must: every minute, each vessel's most recent 12 minutes scored against its own past — no future context. Six-hour replay of the busiest watch, with a key methodological finding: thresholds must be calibrated against the live decision rule, or per-window calibration floods the display (44% of benign tracks firing). Now with behavior-conditional assessment: toggle place-only vs place + recognized behavior — same benign budget, transit vessels tighten (0.88×), and compound alerts flag kinematic surprise on unrecognized-behavior vessels.
15 → 29 alerts behavior-conditioned, same ≤5% watch FAR 2 compound unknown-behavior + kinematic flags
03Multi-hour behaviors (long-timescale arm)
A 256-step, 2-minute-token transformer (~8.5 h context) recognizes what a vessel has been doing for hours: stop-and-go pot/trap tending, slow area-working, shoreline passage, directed transit — clusters fit on the training ports reappear, signatures matching, in the never-seen lower Chesapeake. Long context pays exactly where behavior has long structure; directional look-ahead scene cues make feature-following turns predictable.
rhythmic work: −7.1% NLL from 8× more history fishing-like classes enriched 2.5–3.8×, assigned cold
04Play-forward behavior recognition
The long-horizon model run as it would be used: a full-day causal replay where every 10 minutes each vessel's trailing history (up to 8.5 h, past only) is classified into the learned behavior classes — labels form as evidence accumulates, change with activity, and occasionally fail: unrecognized behavior = the embedding matches no known class, the "no known activity fits" alert.
31,640 causal assessments over 24 h · 407 tracks 480 stable behavior changes · 44 unrecognized-behavior flags
PROTOTYPEPer-(place, activity) dynamics models — the gPC slot
A separate module, deliberately simple: mean-reverting continuous dynamics of speed and turn-rate per (place × activity) context, parameter uncertainty via per-vessel draw mixtures — the interface where generalized polynomial chaos machinery drops in. The entire onboard model is a 64 KB readable table — and a live replay draws each vessel against its context-conditional predictive envelope. On the same Chesapeake watch it fires on sprint-stop derivative anomalies with zero overlap against the transformer's pattern alerts — measured multi-method complementarity.
64 KB total model · 34 (place×activity) contexts 35 alerts, 0 overlap with transformer — complementary coverage
