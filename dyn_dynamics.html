Per-(place, activity) dynamics models

The architecture's third judge, kept deliberately simple: for each (place type × activity class) context, a mean-reverting model of continuous speed and turn-rate — equilibrium speed μ, reversion rate, noise — with parameter uncertainty carried by a mixture over per-vessel parameter draws. This is the interface where a partner's generalized polynomial chaos machinery drops in: same contexts, same conditional-normalcy question, principled uncertainty propagation instead of the draw-mixture stand-in. Fit on Puget Sound + SF Bay; judged on the same cold-start Chesapeake watch as the transformer demos.

The whole model is a readable table

Equilibrium speed μv (kt) per context — fitted where data supports a full (place, activity) fit (bold), otherwise inherited from activity- or place-marginal fallbacks (dim). This table — plus noise and reversion parameters — is the onboard normalcy model: no traffic database, no learned weights.

What it flags on the same watch — and why zero overlap matters

Alerts on the Jun 7 12:00–18:00 Chesapeake slice, same ≤5% benign watch budget as the transformer.

Complementarity, measured. The dynamics judge fired 35 alerts on 20 tracks — with zero overlap (±5 min) against the transformer's alerts on the identical watch. The two watch different aspects of motion: continuous dynamics punish large derivatives (sprint-stops, hard accelerations — mostly fast patrol/pilot craft here), while the token transformer catches pattern anomalies (weaving, creeping, place-inconsistency) that barely move the derivatives. Neither subsumes the other — the multi-method ensemble is the coverage argument. Caveats, honestly: disjointness also means neither validates the other's alerts; several dynamics alerts inherit the stale trailing behavior label (a sprinting pilot boat still labeled "moored"); and the draw-mixture is a placeholder for real gPC uncertainty propagation.
